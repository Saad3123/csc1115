Hobbies

The picture in the right is Taekwondo, an olympic martial art which primarly uses legs in sparring.Athletes wear gears to protect themselves as shown in the picture and yet they get their bones broken, It's a simple example of how powerful our legs can be. I love practicing taekwondo and it's also a my biggest stress reliver. It keeps me stay focused, energitic and clam. I am practicing taekwondo since 3rd grade and have won many state level competitions and I consider taekwondo to be a hobby that keeps me fit.
I was one among the few athletes in our school who won in state level tournaments, our school offered scholarship to me for more than 3 years.

Volunteering is a social hobby for me; it's something I do for enjoyment or when I have spare time. Back in India, I helped organise events in the mosque for special occasions, and my school Pupil Leader title was no different than volunteering service.Serving a community and making people happy is something that brings me joy.
As I am now in Canada, I get to learn a lot of new things every time I serve for an event, which is very interesting. One of the most recent service opportunities I had was on March 30th at VIU. The event was organised by VIUMA, which introduced the Islamic holy holiday Ramadan. I am convinced that volunteering expands my circle of acquaintances and connections.
